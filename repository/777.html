Air Force One: A trip with special M&Ms and James Bond

  • From the section Magazine

It has three floors, a gym and there is always a doctor on board. But getting a ride on Air Force One can be tricky. Only 13 journalists are allowed to travel with the president when he flies, and this week the BBC's Jon Sopel managed to bag a seat on the plane.

As the BBC's North America editor, I travel all the time. From one regional airport to another on American airlines where the best you can hope for is a packet of pretzels.

Americans fly for work like Britons hop on and off buses or trains. Consequently it is utilitarian. Customer service is virtually non-existent. It is grin and bear it travel. Though more grim than grin.

Air Force One is not like that.

Cuba is one of the most exotic places I have visited. The mix of crumbling but beautiful Spanish colonial, 1950s Americana, and 1970s Soviet utilitarian, all laced with plentiful mojito cocktails and the sound of salsa, is beguiling. It's fabulous.

I was here twice last year. The first time was just after Presidents Obama and Castro announced their desire to open a new chapter in relations; the second when Secretary of State John Kerry flew in to re-open the US Embassy on the waterfront.

"There's a special place in hell for women who don't help each other," says former Secretary of State Madeleine Albright. The comment was supposed to help Hillary Clinton but it has also exposed her problem appealing to women voters.

About Jon

Jon's arrival on American shores marks the continuation of a long and distinguished career at the BBC but he's no stranger to Washington, having covered several US elections. And no stranger to the US either, having covered Hurricanes Katrina and Sandy, and recently ridden a Harley Davidson down the West coast - that wasn't work though!

His new role as North America editor comes after a long stint of being one of the lead news anchors in London, presenting on the BBC News Channel, The Politics Show and more recently Global with Jon Sopel. Jon has also presented from around the world on major events - from New York on the tenth anniversary of 9/11, Rome for the installation of the new Pope, South Africa for the funeral of Nelson Mandela, India on their recent elections, and from conflicts in the Middle East, Iraq and Afghanistan. But now he returns to the field to share his thoughts on events in North America.
